Kenya

A Civil society advocacy charter for the coalition was developed and shared on 29 December 2022.

The purpose of the civil society and private sector health financing advocacy consortium is to actively advocate : More money for health – (10% progressive annual increase in the national health budget towards attaining 15% of national budget and 5% of GDP to health), More health for the money (efficiency and better use), Increased equity and financial protection, Improved leadership, governance, and coordination of the health financing process and is aligned to the African Union leadership commitments and the consortium’s joint advocacy plan.

Sierra Leone

Participation in the African Health Agenda International Conference (AHAIC), Kigali, Rwanda 2023

Sierra Leone CSOs being represented by WASH-Net’s Chief Executive Officer, Musa Ansumana Soko, the AHAIC 2023 event focused on an array of themes with climate change and health interface taking center stage in a series of sessions that included two high-level plenary and 14 parallel sessions. Delegates delved into a range of topics including building climate resilience at the community level, leveraging technology to amplify global health, unlocking the power of public health data and closing the geographic and financial divide to empower African researchers. Participants engaged in dialogue that explored both challenges and opportunities for sustainable, unified climate action and investment in health workers, with the consensus being that Africa can - and must - do more to elevate the voices of those working to protect both people and planet and move beyond talk to action. Climate change adaptation and mitigation featured prominently in a rare meeting between World Health Organization (WHO) and the private sector – including pharmaceutical companies, where the need for collective effort in adaptation, and mitigation were discussed. Decarbonizing health system through measures such as proper waste management and industry approach must be prioritized. In other considerations, the need for pharmaceutical manufacturing, genomic data, and harmonized regulatory frameworks were recognized as critical in creating strong mechanisms for pandemic preparedness.

Mozambique

The JLA group’s Civil Society Organizations (CSOs) is co-leading the National Health Financing Dialogue in Mozambique to take place in July 2023.

the JLA groups’ CSO had the opportunity to liaise, network and advocate at an unprecedented level for them as at the National High-Level Dialogue on Health Sector Financing key stakeholders from the Ministry of Health, the Ministry of Economics and Finance, the private sector, parliamentarians (including the Commission of Planning and Budgeting), the Health Partners Group, and civil society from all provinces in the country were present to discuss the Health Financing Strategy of Mozambique with the setting of a clear follow-up plan. Having had the JLA group as a part of this event contributed to this program’s goal of training and support on budget advocacy and accountability in favor of domestic health financing and UHC, with a cadre of resource people that is capable of delivering training on domestic health financing, UHC and budget advocacy.

Tanzania

Meaningful engagement and participation in the country/national dialogues (physical and virtual) to create awareness for the Single National Health Insurance.

Through our engagement with African College of Insurance and Social Protection, and other likeminded institutions, the coalition hosted several consultative meetings in collaboration with Ministry of Health to create awareness of various institution on UHI Bill and their responsibilities towards acceleration of the community enrolment to upon approval by the Parliament. These coalition hosted two major meetings between the Ministry of Health, Health Training Institutions and Faith Based Leaders. Both meetings were attended by Minister of Health and other ministry officials who provided presentations on the proposed bill and captured inputs from the participants. The meeting supported to improve the review of the Bill in various articles including issues related to pro poor financing mechanism, retention of community health insurance scheme and change the name to universal primary health insurance scheme, establishment of the equity fund to support the implementation of the scheme and the needs for reviewing the premium package. On the same, MoH in collaboration with the coalition established a social media account in Twitter @bima_afyatz dedicated for UHI awareness creation foster and a platform to foster national dialogues.
